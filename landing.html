About Me

"We are made of starstuff." - Carl Sagan

Hi! I’m Harum Ahmed, a third-year Ph.D. candidate in Physics at the University of North Texas, where I work with Dr. Ohad Shemmer to study some of the most distant and powerful objects in the universe, quasars. These active supermassive black holes are among the brightest, most energetic phenomena ever discovered, blazing to life just 1.6 to 4.5 billion years after the Big Bang and offering a glimpse 85–90% of the way back to the dawn of time.

I was born and raised in Pakistan, before moving to the U.S. in 2013. I earned both my M.Sc. (2025) and B.Sc. (2023) in Physics from University of North Texas.

When I’m not diving into the cosmos, you’ll find me nose-deep in a good book--romance, sci-fi, fantasy, and of course, anything Harry Potter (always waiting on my Hogwarts letter). I also love writing poetry and collecting little adventures around the world.

About Me

"We are made of starstuff." - Carl Sagan

My name is Harum Ahmed and I am a third-year Ph.D. Astrophysics candidate at the University of North Texas working with Dr. Ohad Shemmer. My research involves studying highly distant quasars (active supermassive black holes) 1.6 to 4.5 billion years after the Big Bang — that’s over 85–90% of the way back to the beginning of the universe.

I was born and raised in Pakistan, before moving to the U.S. in 2013. I obtained a M.Sc. in Physics in 2025 and a B.Sc. in Physics in 2023, both from University of North Texas.

Besides black holes, I enjoy reading books (romance, sci-fi, fantasy), traveling and writing poetry.
